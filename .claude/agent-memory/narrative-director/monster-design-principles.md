**Core rule (from game-pillars.md):** "What ordinary workplace or public-service pressure became physical here?" Name the job title first, then what broke.

**Design test:** Every monster must pass: (1) identifiable civilian/bureaucratic job title, (2) a specific institutional rule they are still executing, (3) a reason that rule is now meaningless or harmful, (4) a sensory signature (silhouette + sound motif + color mark).

**Horror register:** "制度的尸体" (corpse of the institution) — not gore, not jump scares. Fear comes from recognizing a system still running with no one left to care.

**Amber-orange eye convention:** Current threat signal standard in the game's visual language. Can be inherited or subverted, but subversion requires narrative justification (e.g., candidate 3's scanner indicator).

**Established monsters:**
- 感染监理 (Tower EarthCoast-01): safety inspector still executing a never-completed acceptance checklist. Faded hi-vis yellow. Rhythmic clipboard-tapping. **Recommended for this map.**
- 欠薪工 (candidate, not yet assigned): construction worker waiting for a restart order that will never come. Cement-gray, no color mark — blends into walls.
- 封锁程序执行体 (candidate, future 贵客瘟 truth-thread maps): government quarantine officer executing a lockdown order that was itself a cover-up. Blue-white uniform + amber scanner light near the 生态柱.

**Why:** Institutional horror aligns with the Municipal Debt Noir pillar and differentiates Black Commission from creature-feature extraction games.
