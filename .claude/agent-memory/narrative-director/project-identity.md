**Setting:** 2098. Mars colonized by the wealthy; Earth left to low-credit, indebted populations. Fire-class pathogen MRC-7 (贵客瘟) brought back by Mars returnees devastated Earth's ecology — officially blamed on "Earth's own ecological collapse."

**Player role:** Employees of a near-bankrupt 地表采回事务所, taking dangerous extraction jobs for Mars clients who want "real Earth" objects they can't synthesize.

**Core irony:** Earth people risk death to collect Earth's remnants, which Mars clients consume as luxury goods.

**Tone:** Municipal Debt Noir. Satire is delivered through contracts, settlement screens, and client feedback — never through narration. "The contract speaks."

**Color palette:** dead-rubber black, concrete gray, aged paper, warm tungsten amber (inhabitation signal), civic teal, sodium amber, dispatch green, stamp red.

**Signature loop:** HQ office → computer → gear → van dispatch → mission site → objective/partial return choice → settlement.

**Progression:** Five license stages are the backbone, not XP bars. Progress = deeper complicity.

**Why:** All narrative work should reinforce the debt-noir irony and never tip into hero-narrative or explicit moral judgment. The system condemns itself.
